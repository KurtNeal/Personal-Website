ABOUT ME

Kurt Neal Lorenz Villalongja Cabanig

Software Engineer, Digital Artist, and visual development artist

Hello! my name is Kurt Cabanig a disabled person from the Philippines, I am a Software Engineer for a year, and a Digital Artist for over three years.

Creating and designing unique yet fun designs, artworks, and programs have driven me to become an artist/programmer in spite of my disease called hemophilia. Despite the challenges that come with my condition, I’ve learned to see my disability not as a limitation, but as a source of strength and creativity.

It has taught me patience, perseverance, and the importance of adaptability—qualities that I bring into every project I create. Whether I’m coding a new software/application, refining features in a program, or painting a digital artwork, I aim to blend functionality with artistic expression, creating experiences that are not only visually engaging but also accessible to everyone disabled or not.

Through my work, I seek to challenge assumptions about what individuals with disabilities can achieve, proving that creativity and innovation thrive in the face of adversity. My journey has shaped my perspective as both an artist and a programmer, urging me to approach life's problems with empathy and clear intention. Each project to me is an opportunity to tell a story, solve a problem, and inspire others—especially those facing similar challenges—to pursue their passions. By embracing both my abilities and condition, I continue to grow, create, and contribute meaningful work that reflects resilience, inclusivity, and imagination.
